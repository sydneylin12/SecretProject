The journey to Flora was the most silent they had ever been on one of these long walks between towns. And that was entirely Astero's fault. 

He could feel Rosella looking at him, perhaps wanting to say something, but ultimately, she would fall silent as well. Her eyes were full of worry, and Astero hated that he was upsetting her, but he could not bring himself to speak. 

I am also from the sky. Just consider it a favor.

The man had looked a bit familiar, but Astero had never known anyone by the name Will. He might have been like Rosella: someone sent to the ground for committing a crime, in which case, Astero had even more cause for suspicion. 

But how could he have known Astero was also from the sky? 

Astero looked down at himself. All the clothes he wore were bought on the ground. Same for his boots. The satchel he wore was quite popular amongst the ground folk. He even tied his hair back so he did not stand out too much. What gave him away? 

Unless... 

Was he talking about Rosella?

Astero banished the thought. Impossible. 

Maybe he had been from the Elessai clan and recognized Astero. But he could not remember the Elessai clan sending anyone to the ground in all the time he had been there. He could have been from another clan. Or maybe it was the way Astero carried himself. Were his shoulders too rigid, his posture screaming nobility? He ought to blend in more by slouching— 

"Shall we rest for a bit?" 

Rosella's voice startled Astero out of his jumbled thoughts. She hardly ever suggested they rest, able to walk for miles without stopping. Astero was always the one needing to rest his feet, to stop for water, or to wipe the sweat from his neck. He only recalled Rosella ever stopping to peer at flowers along the side of the road. 

He looked over at her and saw concern written all over her face. It was obvious she was not requesting to stop for herself, but for him. 

"I'm all right," Astero assured, giving her what he hoped looked like a smile, although it may have come out as a grimace. 

She did not look convinced. "We are stopping." 

Astero sighed as Rosella moved to the side of the road, taking a seat on the dry grass. She simply stared at him, raising a challenging brow as if daring him to question her. He gulped, for he had never seen this sort of demeanor from her. Realizing he had no choice, Astero went and took a seat next to her. 

A cool summer breeze blew by, and the scent of the dusty air filled Astero's nose. He was not used to this kind of environment. With the Elessai manor close to the Pearl Sea, the aroma of salty mist and sand wafted through the manor walls. It was the fragrance of home. Even the Fen clan manor sat right on top of a lake. He was used to the moisture in the air. 

The dryness of the area surrounding him now made him feel sick, and he desperately wanted to leave. 

"I think I am done resting," Astero declared, already ready to stand. 

Rosella made no move. Astero had never seen her like this, with eyes so empty she looked like a corpse. 

Immediately, he crouched down beside her, taking her face in his hands. He finally realized she had not smiled once since last night, and that thought alone pained Astero more than he could bear. "Are you all right?" 

She pushed his hands away, and Astero recoiled as if she had slapped him. 

When she spoke, her voice was cold. "I told you I was from the sky." 

Unsure with what to do with his hands, Astero settled for placing them in his lap. They shook unsteadily. "You did." 

"I trusted you with that secret." Rosella looked at him, her brown eyes glowing bright beneath the sunlight. "Can you not do the same?" 

The weight of her gaze was suffocating. Astero closed his eyes. "Will you just trust me that everything is all right?" 

He could lie to her. He could say that the man said his house was unkempt, or that he was trying to impress Rosella, or someone had died in that house and everyone stayed away for fear of ghosts and the undead. But he did not want to lie to her. He had told her so many lies already.

Astero cursed himself for being so stricken by Will's words and putting himself in this position in the first place. 

"You want me to trust you," Rosella stated, "but you don't trust me?" 

"I do!" Astero assured. 

But suddenly, Astero was struck with the realization that he did not actually trust Rosella at all. 

He did not trust her to forgive him if he told her who he was. He did not trust her to stay with him if she knew what he had done, that he was the boy who abandoned her. Astero could never show his true self to Rosella. 

Astero chastised himself. Another lie.

"It is a complicated matter, and I must keep it to myself. It only concerns me, I promise." This, at least, was the truth. 

She stared at him for a few moments, and Astero felt like shrinking underneath her scrutiny. Then, she just shook her head and sighed. "I'll let go of the matter for now." 

He did not like that she looked so unhappy. If he were the cause of her unhappiness, what was the point in coming down here to be with her? Maybe the best thing he could do for Rosella was to stay away from her, for all he seemed to bring into her life was misery. 

The two made their way back to the road, traveling in silence, the whistling wind being the only sound in Astero's ears. He spent that time thinking of ways to help her once they settled down in Flora. For one, he would go on every single job she went on. He would also take jobs of his own and give all his earnings to her. Astero would buy Rosella anything she wanted or anything he thought she would like. He would learn to cook, and he would cook for her so she would never have to step foot in the kitchen again. He would do the cleaning. He would take care of the expenses. He would do everything. 

Throughout his years of living in the sky, Astero often wondered what he was good for. After years of failing to do anything noteworthy in his innovative clan, after failing to be a dependable son to his mother and father, after constantly failing to find Rosella for years, Astero came to the conclusion that there was indeed nothing he was good for. 

On the other hand, Rosella had more potential than he ever could. She was born a noble woman, kind and caring and selfless beyond compare. She should have been the lady of a prestigious clan, leading her people to prosper. Perhaps she would have married Helik Istora and become Lady Istora, ruling the largest clan in the sky. Rosella could have been so much more than someone working her life away on the ground. 

Astero kept throwing worries glances towards Rosella, but her gaze remained straight ahead, her eyes hard. 

He sighed, an ache in his heart. 

"Can I tell you something?" Astero asked quietly. 

Rosella side-eyed him suspiciously. "What is it?" 

"I grew up in an orphanage somewhere in the north." His hands balled into fists, his nails digging into his hands. "They did not treat the children well, so I ran away when I was five years old and came across Ludha where I lived on the streets." 

She remained silent, but her eyes were more curious now. 

"Those were not easy years." Even now, Astero could hear the snap of his arm. He could feel every blow to his body and every wad of spit thrown his way. He remembered hands all over him, his wrists gripped so tight he could not feel his fingers. If he closed his eyes, he found himself drowning again, his head held down in a bucket full of water, his voice screaming but unable to make a sound. His empty stomach never satiated, his hair never combed, his clothes never washed. 

One did not forget such a time in their life. 

"I was tormented and scorned by everyone. Ludha did not have a proper orphanage, but I would sometimes stay with others. I was rarely treated well." Astero tried to breathe, but hardly any air made its way to his lungs. He swallowed hard, forcing himself to keep speaking. "I was a starving, beaten, homeless orphan who did not even have the courage to leave that place for fear other villages would only be the same." 

Astero did not realize he had been shaking until Rosella took his hand in her own, steady and warm. 

If telling her some form of the truth was what it took to make her happy again, he needed to do so. 

"Then, my mother found me." The thought of his mother made Astero's heart fill with warmth. "She took me in." 

"Every child deserves a home." Rosella patted his hand gently. "Your mother must have believed that." 

"I do not know if I deserved her kindness." 

"Of course you did," she insisted. "Why do you not think so?" 

There were so many reasons. So many he could not say. 

But he just shrugged. "I was not a good child. But since then, I have lived a carefree, easy life." 

"You still had your fair share of hardships," Rosella pointed out. "Being forced to marry someone you hate, for one." 

He shrugged. Being forced to marry Melody was not exactly something he considered a hardship. It was more akin to a thorn in his side, or an insect bite on his neck. Annoying and irritating, but incomparable to what he faced before. 

And, he would imagine, incomparable to what Rosella had endured all these years. 

"But I was still lucky," he replied. 

She looked to be in thought. "You seem to be a lucky person."  

It was indeed luck that his mother found him. It was luck that Rosella found him. And luckily, he was able to find her. Perhaps the moment Rosella took his hand fifteen years ago in the Ozario clan garden, all her good fortune transferred to him, leaving her with nothing. 

Rosella had told him of her life in the sky the day of their picnic in the meadow. She told him her full name was Rosella Ozario. She lived in a manor with her parents and siblings. She studied different flowers and spent her days in the garden. There was so much she unveiled about her life. He could not give her much in return, but he would try. 

The two fell into silence once more, but Rosella's mood seemed to have lifted. 

After a few long hours, Astero smelled a fresh fragrance in the air. It seemed familiar, this scent of flowers. 

"We're almost there!" Rosella exclaimed, picking up her pace. 

Astero did not realize how much his legs felt they were about to crumble until he tried to keep up with her. 

"R - Rosella!" he called, breathing heavily, his vision turning foggy. Astero tried to blink the blurriness away to no avail. He shook his head violently, trying to clear his sight and his mind, but all he could see was Rosella's shadow running back to him before he fell. 

Astero could barely breathe. The merciless sun made his head spin, and now that they were in the east, it seemed even worse. His sweat made his clothes cling to his body, making him feel trapped. 

The tip of the water jug he and Rosella bought back in the lake town was brought to his mouth. Rosella gently tilted his head back, and lukewarm water flowed down his throat. Although not quite refreshing, it did make him feel slightly better. 

"We should have taken more breaks," he heard Rosella mutter. 

Astero felt like such a burden. He wanted to tell her to just leave him there, but words would not come out amidst his dry throat. 

"Come on," Rosella grunted as she hoisted him up off the ground. Astero tried his best to stay upright, but he ended up having to lean on her pathetically. 

As they kept walking to Flora, Rosella sighed. "You must have been so tired. Why did you not say anything?" 

He could only groan in response. All Astero wanted to do was lie down and close his eyes, but he fought against that useless part of him, forced himself to keep putting one foot in front of the other. 

"We're almost there," Rosella assured. "Do you smell the flowers?" 

"Mm," he responded weakly in affirmation. 

"I'll start a new life here," Rosella declared, her voice brimming with hope. "The perfect town for me." 

Astero nodded in agreement. "Perfect," he murmured. 

Rosella stopped to give him more water, and Astero began to feel much better. 

She sighed. "We should have bought horses." 

They continued to walk, with Astero slowly becoming able to breathe again, his vision returning as well. When he finally found the strength to walk without Rosella's support, he heard the bustling sounds of a town. They turned the corner, the last stretch of the path, and gazed upon Flora. 

Will's words had been true. It was an endless town full of flowers. 

As expected, Rosella was speechless. 

Then again, so was Astero. Flora seemed to be so vibrant, filled with so much life that it had a beating heart of its own. A myriad of colors painted the town, with a variety of flowers and trees and clothing choices. It wasn't like a town in the sky, where one particular color dominated the rest. Flora was like if all the clans in the sky came together to create one big town, where all their colors and cultures clashed. 

Shrubs lined the edges of the town, and stones lined the pathsways throughout the entire area, with mini gardens along the sides. There were probably more people here than in Sila, although quite a few of them had to just be visitors. The locals were watering the plants or managing the shops. Children ran through the fields and played in the grass. Flora radiated a sort of energy that could make anyone smile. Under the bright summer sun, it looked like paradise. 

If there were more pink, this would probably be what the towns in the Ozario clan looked like back then. 

Astero turned to Rosella, and judging by the tears in her eyes, he knew she was thinking the same thing. 

He took her hand, and together, they made their way up the path to Flora, where a new life awaited them.
