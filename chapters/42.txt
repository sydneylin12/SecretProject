Out of the many towns Astero and Rosella passed by on their way to the east, this one was certainly the most beautiful. Although the lakeside town had its charm, Sila had an extravagant night life. 

The two of them arrived late in the evening, when the sky was at its clearest and the stars brighter than ever. It was a small town just at the edge of the eastern region, but it looked the most lively out of them all. 

Music could be heard for miles. At the center of the town, people were dancing, clapping, stomping their feet to the music. All the lanterns lit up the night, and the town glowed in the firelight, as if all the stars were right here with them. Rosella felt as if she had stepped into another universe. 

"Wow," she breathed. 

Beside her, Astero nodded, equally in awe. 

The town square was filled with everyone, from young children to the elderly, all entranced in the lively band, dancing as if the world would end tomorrow. 

Her journey from the north to the east in just two weeks was more eventful than the months she spent making her way to the north from the west. Back then, she had been traveling because she was kicked out of the village or could not find a place to stay. Trying to survive, she slowly made her way north, staying in villages or towns for months or years at a time. There had to have been much to see along that journey, so why couldn't she remember anything good about it? Despite the same thing happening, despite being banished from Onen, this journey had been so much more enjoyable. 

A hand grabbed hers and starting pulling her into the dance circle. She thought it was Astero, but then she saw him being pulled along as well. They looked at each other, both momentarily shocked, before the two burst out laughing. Rosella began stomping her feet to the music, following her partner's lead. All the tiredness from the walking, all the stressing of wondering if they would find a real place to stay, all the worries she had of anything at all, left her body through her feet as she danced them all away. Of course, as a lady of a prestigious clan, she learned how to dance at a young age, and although this type of dance was more energetic and unrestrained, her body moved along to it naturally. As the dancing progressed, she would move along to another partner, smiling and connecting with them despite saying no words. She danced with everyone, underneath the glowing lanterns, underneath the night sky, and Rosella could not recall the last time her heart beat so fast, like it would burst out of her chest. 

Across the dance floor, she made eye contact with a familiar pair of eyes, and Rosella danced her way over there, unable to contain her smile. He waited for her, and when she approached, he placed one hand on her waist, and held her hand in his other. He quickly brought them back into the circle, and Rosella threw her head back in laughter, not expecting he could dance as well. Rosella twirled and dipped and jumped, and she saw his eyes sparkling like a pair of stars that only shined on her. 

The music picked up, and everyone clapped to the beat as the dancers moved faster. Rosella squealed in delight as Astero spun them faster, and she could barely see him as the scenery before her eyes moved too fast, but she felt his warm hands on her, the two of them moving like one body. 

And she realized this journey could only be so enjoyable with him there. 

When the song ended, Astero and Rosella stepped away from the crowd. She wiped the sweat from her forehead, trying to regain her breath. Rosella turned to Astero in amazement. "I did not know you could dance!" 

He laughed, the sound filling Rosella's heart with warmth. "I am not nearly as good as you." 

"False," she proclaimed, pointing an accusing finger right at him. "You were a rich man. Of course you were involved in the high-class dancing scene." 

"And where, pray tell, did you learn?" Astero asked with a smirk, leaning in closer.

Rosella grinned. "I'll have you know, I used to be a prestigious lady." She pointed to the sky. "Up there. 

Astero's face suddenly fell, his eyes turning sad unexpectedly. "Used to," he said, so quietly Rosella almost did not hear him. 

She smiled sadly. "The past is past." Rosella placed a gentle, reassuring hand on his cheek. "Please do not pity me." 

He shook his head, reaching up a hand to grasp hers. "You are still the most noble, honorable woman there is." 

Rosella laughed in surprise. Those words sounded so bizarre, so absurdly untrue. And yet, there was a part of her that almost believed him. There was a part of her that wanted it to be true. 

Astero looked determined now. He grasped both her hands tightly in between them, like he wanted her to feel how much he meant it. "I — "

They both jumped as a new song started. People flocked to the dance floor, and the festivities began again. 

Rosella wondered what he was going to say, but before she could inquire, Astero asked, "Do you want to find a place to stay?"

She nodded, and together, they strolled through the busy streets. His hand still held onto hers, and she couldn't help but smile. 

The lively market spanned multiple streets, and Rosella truly believed not a single person was asleep in their home. It was as crowded as a normal marketplace in the daytime. Even children roamed the town, playing with each other or walking by their parents. When Rosella and Astero saw this place from afar as they were arriving, it looked as if it were another world, a dreamland amidst the dark of the night. 

"Do you want to stay here?" Astero asked. 

She turned and saw him looking at her, studying her face. 

"You seem to like it here," he pointed out. 

Rosella shrugged, scanning the town. She did like the lively night, the cheerful music, the endless laughter. It was a lovely place, but it could not be for her. "I do," she replied, "but I would not want to live here. It would be nice to visit from time to time, but I can't imagine always working at night." 

He nodded in understanding. "Should we leave tomorrow, then?" 

"What's the next city on the map?" 

Astero reached into his shoulder bag and pulled out the map, unfolding it so they could both look. A few more miles, and the next city they would come across was a place called...

"Flora?" they said at the same time. The two looked at each other curiously. 

One of the passerby suddenly stopped in front of them. A man, whose hair was as dark as Astero's, but whose eyes were the brightest blue Rosella had ever seen. He approached them with a smile. "Are you two lost?" 

Rosella looked at Astero, but he was staring at the man strangely, as if trying to remember where they had met before. 

Seeing that he wasn't going to say anything, or he was too busy scrutinizing the stranger, Rosella replied. "Yes, umm, do you know of a town called Flora?" 

The man nodded. "Just a few more miles east of here. Home of the famous Angel Gardens. The place is full of flowers, hence the name." 

Immediately, Rosella lit up. "Is it a big town?" 

"Bigger than this one, that's for sure." 

"Perfect!" She turned to Astero, who was still staring. "Flora might be a good place to stay." 

He blinked, looking down at her, then back at the man, then back to her. Astero smiled nervously. "Wherever you want to stay, we will stay." 

"Looking for a place to live?" the man asked curiously. 

She nodded. "Do you know anywhere we can stay in Flora for a long time?" 

The man smiled. "You're in luck. I actually have a house there that I'm not using. Would you like to stay there?" 

Rosella gasped in delight. "We would love to!"

Before she could say more, Astero cut in. "What's the cost?" he asked, seeming very skeptical. 

The man shook his head. "I own a lot of homes in these eastern towns, and I travel too much to stay in one place. Consider the house yours, and just let me stay there when I'm in the area." 

It did seem too good to be true. Why would a man they just met do something so nice for them? 

"There's nothing in this for you," Astero pointed out. 

"You two will be watching over one of my houses for me."

"It is still not a fair deal." 

Sighing, the man leaned close to Astero and whispered something in his ear. Rosella could not hear a thing, but Astero's eyes widened, all sorts of emotions running through them. Panic, fear, surprise, shock. 

"Relax." The man clapped Astero on the shoulder. "Let me draw a layout of the place so you can easily find it." 

The man took the map and made his way to a nearby table outside an eatery, flipping it so he could draw on the other side. 

"Astero?" Rosella tugged on his arm. "Is everything all right?" 

His face was white, as if he had seen a ghost. 

"Did he threaten you?" Rosella demanded, feeling a sudden burst of anger. 

Astero shook his head slowly, his eyes unfocused. 

"What happened?" 

"Umm... he..." Astero cleared his throat nervously. "He was just being generous." 

"You don't expect me to believe that, do you?" Rosella asked incredulously. 

Astero finally turned to her, forcing a reassuring smile on his face. "Just trust me. Everything is fine." 

"It did not look fine at all." What could the man possibly have said? Rosella could not even come up with possibilities. And it appeared that while Astero was asking her to trust him, he did not trust her enough to explain.

Before Astero could say anything, the man began strolling back to them, the map in hand. With a friendly smile on his face, he handed the map back to Astero. Then, he handed a key to Rosella, presumably for the house. "Apologies, I only have one key." 

Rosella took the key carefully. "Thank you." She could not bring herself to feel at ease about the situation, but all she could do was trust Astero. 

"You can't miss it." The man smiled at Astero, but all Astero did was look down at the map, his expression empty. Rosella wanted to reach out and comfort him, but she was also at a loss. 

"Thank you, sir," said Rosella. "And what is your name?" 

"The name's Will!" he replied cheerfully, already turning away. "Enjoy the rest of the evening!" He waved goodbye as he walked away. Rosella watched his retreating figure go until he was completely out of sight. 

In all her years, Rosella had never been a lucky person. Most of the people who let her stay with them either wanted her in their bed, or they wanted to extract as much money from her as possible. Very few had been genuinely nice, letting her stay in exchange for mere housework. But Rosella ended up trusting those people too much, and as she disclosed her secrets, they turned her away. 

She could not believe she and Astero had gotten so lucky. 

"Promise me everything is okay?" Rosella asked. 

Folding the map and placing it back in his bag, Astero nodded calmly. The fiery lanterns illuminated his face, casting an uncertain glow. "Let's find a room for the night."
